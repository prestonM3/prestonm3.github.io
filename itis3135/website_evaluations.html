Website Evaluations

C.R.A.P Design Principles - An Acronym for Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity. These help build quality sites with consistency.

pnwx.com

Poorly Designed

  • Contrast: The bright and inconsistent colors make it hard for distinction amongst items and navigation.
  • Repetition: Along with contrast, the repetition in general is inconsistent. Margins differ, colors differ, theres no repetitive rule for clickable items, etc.
  • Alignment: Pictures have different margins amongst each other, UI elements feel randomly placed and aren't consistent, making it hard for the viewer to find a standing on the site.
  • Proximity: Related items such as sizing charts with the applicable items aren't together. Additional information for items are not together, where some are toward the bottom of the item causing inconsistent reading for the viewer.

airbnb.com

Well Designed

  • Contrast: Colors are strategically used on the website. The thumbnails for the listings have emphasized colors due to the minimized background. This creates a guided environment for the user.
  • Repetition: The contrast principle is maintained throughout the website, where important items such as the search nav logo being a bright red to guide the user to the search bar. Text don't overwhelm the users eye due to consistency. Logos for tabs are also consistent in the idea of being an "important" item.
  • Alignment: Thumbnails for listings are aligned in rows, guiding the users to look to the right for more listings. This creates an intentionally environment for aligned items. Filters also follow the same idea of the aligned row, which freely shows the options the user has for filtering listings.
  • Proximity: Listings are grouped together since they are related. Specific listings however, such as Panama City Beach listings, are grouped under that heading. This creates organization on the website.
